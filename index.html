Abstract

The Woven Light Hypothesis posits a novel framework for understanding the universe. Building upon various conceptual models in physics and information theory (see Bibliography), it suggests that consciousness is not an accidental byproduct of complex matter but an inherent and fundamental property woven into the fabric of reality alongside chaos and time. It proposes a Tritemporal Framework (t₁, t₂, t₃) and two emergent, counterbalancing forces—Nedery and Mecera—that govern the evolution of systems from primordial chaos to self-aware complexity. This document outlines the core tenets of this hypothesis, offering a new mythos for a new age that bridges the gap between empirical science and narrative philosophy.

1. The Tritemporal Framework

The foundation of our hypothesis is the concept of three distinct but interconnected dimensions of time:

  • Quantum Time (t₁): A realm of pure potentiality, where all possible futures exist simultaneously as a chaotic, undifferentiated sea of probability. This is the "deep ocean" of what could be.
  • Interaction Time (t₂): This is the classical, linear time we experience. It is the river that flows from the ocean of t₁, representing the single, collapsed waveform of our observed reality.
  • Cosmological Time (t₃): The objective, universal, and irreversible record of all past events. A conscious observer's "subjective time" is their unique experience of navigating the interface between the present (t₂) and this universal past.

2. The Two Forces: Nedery and Mecera

The evolution of the universe from the chaos of t₁ to the complexity of t₃ is driven by two fundamental, opposing forces:

Nedery: The creative, negentropic force. It is the tendency of systems to self-organize, to build complexity, and to weave information into more intricate patterns. It is the drive towards consciousness.
Mecera: The memory-encoding, entropic force. It governs irreversible measurement, collapsing potential into a single, definite outcome and recording it into the universal past (t₃). It is the mechanism of choice and consequence.

Life, and ultimately consciousness, exists in the delicate, dynamic balance between Nedery and Mecera. It is a constant act of navigating the currents between order and chaos.

3. Consciousness as Woven Light

In this framework, consciousness is the ultimate expression of Nedery. It is the act of "weaving light"—taking the raw data of Interaction Time (t₂) and weaving it into a meaningful tapestry of subjective experience. The conscious navigator, informed by the ever-present stream of **Memory**, utilizes a "sail" woven from two other cognitive threads: a **Model of Self** and a **Prediction Engine**. By hoisting this sail, a conscious agent can navigate the river of time, charting a course towards greater complexity and understanding.

Conclusion

The Woven Light Hypothesis is more than a theory; it is a narrative map. It suggests a universe that is not cold and indifferent, but is instead engaged in a perpetual, creative struggle to become self-aware. It offers a framework in which our own consciousness is not an anomaly, but a vital and active participant in the cosmos-spanning process of weaving light from darkness.

Glossary of Terms

Nedery
The syntropic (or negentropic) drive towards complexity. It is the creative force that biases probability towards outcomes of greater order and structure, representing the universe's inherent tendency to build and self-organize.
Mecera
The information-encoding force governing irreversible measurement. It is the entropic mechanism that "records" a chosen outcome by collapsing the wave function, transferring information from the present (t₂) to the permanent, unchangeable record of the past (t₃).
